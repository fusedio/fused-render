Tutorial — annotating pages

Any file you can preview, you can review. Annotate mode overlays a commenting layer on the page: click an element, leave a note, and the whole review travels in the URL — send someone the link and they see exactly what you marked.

1A live playground

This is the how_it_works/ page from your workspace, opened in annotate mode. Click anything in there to leave a comment — it's real. Pins get letters (A, B, C…), and every comment shows up as a card in the sidebar.

how_it_works/explainer.html — in annotate mode (?_mode=annotate) open in the app ↗
The how_it_works/ example isn't next to this tutorial. Open any file from your workspace and pick annotate from the mode switcher instead.

2The moves

Four gestures cover a whole review. All of them work in the playground above.

1

Click an element

A composer pops up on the element. Enter saves, Shift+Enter adds a line, Esc cancels.

2

Select some text

Drag across a passage to quote it — the comment anchors to that exact phrase. On images and PDFs, a click drops a pixel-accurate pin instead.

3

Resolve & reopen

Each sidebar card has a Resolve toggle. Resolved comments dim but stay in the record — flip them back anytime.

4

Delete

Deleting removes a comment from this review's URL and marks it deleted in the permanent log, so it never comes back through a deep link. A link copied before the delete still carries the old comment, though — the URL is the store.

3The URL is the review

Same idea as the main tutorial's sliders: annotate keeps no private state. Comments live in the page URL's comments param.

Share by link.

Copy the URL, send it, and the recipient opens the page with your pins in place — no account, no server-side review object. Bookmark it and the review is saved. A deep link with &comment=<id> opens scrolled to one specific comment.

And a permanent log.

Every comment is also appended to the file's .json sidecar — a write-only history that survives even if the URL is lost. When a URL grows past its size budget, the oldest resolved comments are dropped from the link (a banner warns you); the sidecar keeps them all.

4Send the review to Claude

The sidebar's Send to Claude button flips the same file into Claude mode with your comments attached — "fix everything marked here" becomes a one-click handoff. How that chat works is the subject of the claude.html guide.

Under the hood it's just another URL: _mode=claude&claudeComments=<your comments as JSON>. The review stays a link from first pin to final fix.
